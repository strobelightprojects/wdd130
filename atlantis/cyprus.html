Why Do people think Cyprus couldn't be the location?

Cyprus, located in the eastern Mediterranean, has been inhabited since the Neolithic period, and was an important center of trade and commerce in the ancient world. Archaeological evidence from the Bronze Age suggests that the island was home to a sophisticated civilization with a well-developed culture and economy. Supporters of the theory that Cyprus is the Lost City of Atlantis point to the island's geography as evidence of its connection to the legendary civilization. Cyprus is surrounded by a series of circular bays, which some argue could be interpreted as the concentric rings of water and land described by Plato. Additionally, the island's central mountain range could be seen as the "central island" of Atlantis.

Why Do people think Cyprus could be the location?

The theory that Cyprus is the lost city of Atlantis has been proposed by some, but it is not widely accepted by scholars and experts for several reasons. There is a lack of supporting evidence to back up the theory. There is no mention of Cyprus in Plato's dialogues, nor is there any archaeological evidence to suggest that the island was home to an advanced civilization in ancient times. The timeframe of Atlantis is believed to be around 9,000 years before Plato's time, which is much earlier than the known history of Cyprus. While the island has a long and rich history, its recorded history dates back only to around 1,400 BC, which is much later than the timeframe of Atlantis. Also,the geographical location of Cyprus is not consistent with Plato's description of Atlantis. Plato described Atlantis as being beyond the "Pillars of Hercules," which are thought to be the Strait of Gibraltar. This would place Atlantis in the Atlantic Ocean, rather than the Mediterranean where Cyprus is located.
